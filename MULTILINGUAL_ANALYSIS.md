# Multilingual Dataset Impact Analysis

## Research Question
**Does adding a multilingual dataset help improve the research?**

## Current Dataset Scope
- **Afri-Names**: African-accented English (8 accents)
- **AfriSpeech-Dialog**: African-accented English (6 accents)
- **Total**: ~490 clips, all English
- **Focus**: Accent variation within English

## Potential Multilingual Additions

### 1. African Languages
- **Swahili** (East Africa, ~100M speakers)
- **Yoruba** (West Africa, ~50M speakers)
- **Amharic** (Ethiopia, ~25M speakers)
- **Hausa** (West Africa, ~80M speakers)
- **Zulu** (South Africa, ~12M speakers)

### 2. Code-Switching Samples
- English + African language mixed speech
- Common in African communication
- Realistic usage patterns

### 3. Multilingual Speech Datasets
- CommonVoice (Mozilla)
- FLEURS (Google)
- VoxPopuli (Facebook)

## Research Benefits

### 1. **Broader Generalization**
- Test codec robustness across language families
- Identify language-specific codec performance
- Understand cross-linguistic transfer

### 2. **Enhanced Research Impact**
- More comprehensive evaluation
- Addresses real-world multilingual scenarios
- Appeals to broader research community

### 3. **Novel Insights**
- Language-specific codec preferences
- Phonetic feature preservation across languages
- Cultural/linguistic factors in codec performance

### 4. **Publication Value**
- Stronger research contribution
- More compelling story
- Broader audience appeal

## Technical Considerations

### 1. **Dataset Availability**
- **Pros**: Many African language datasets exist
- **Cons**: Varying quality and size
- **Solution**: Curate high-quality subsets

### 2. **Evaluation Complexity**
- **Pros**: Richer analysis possibilities
- **Cons**: More complex experimental design
- **Solution**: Stratified analysis by language

### 3. **Computational Cost**
- **Pros**: More comprehensive evaluation
- **Cons**: Longer processing time
- **Solution**: Phased evaluation approach
